Common objections + responses

The 7 questions you'll hear most often, with the honest answer ready. Don't memorize verbatim — internalize the shape.

"We're already on [Square / Mindbody / RGP / etc.] and it works."
"Totally fair. The question isn't whether it works — it's whether the friction it adds is costing you more than $5-20k a year in lost weekend revenue or front-desk hours. A 1-hour Spike conversation costs you nothing and either confirms you're fine, or surfaces something. Either way you win."
"We tried custom software once and got burned."
"Same. Most agencies bill 8-week discovery cycles, deliver in 6 months, and disappear. Our shape is different: $5k for a 1-week prototype, walk away free if you don't like it by day 3. You're not betting anything meaningful upfront."
"What about ongoing support?"
"30 days of post-ship support is included on Production Sprint at no extra cost. After that, no retainer required — we're a side project, not a managed-services firm. If you want a small fix later, $3k Fix-it Sprint covers a week. We deliberately don't sell ongoing IT contracts."
"How is this different from an offshore Upwork dev?"
"Same hours, different outcome. The work is done by us — Doug ships, Jon does the security review — in the same time zone, in person if needed. And every project includes a working AI-arbitration clause via DecisionLayer that an Upwork dev wouldn't know exists."
"What's this DecisionLayer thing — sounds risky."
"Fair question. It's an arbitration clause — same legal concept as the one in your AT&T or Uber TOS, but the dispute gets resolved via an AI-mediated review process at ~$500 in 45-90 days instead of $100k+ in court. We can show you a simulated case run on our own waivers where the system ruled for the operator. Worst case you don't include it. No lock-in."
"How do I know you'll deliver if it's a side project?"
"Because we ship daily, you see progress every day, and we eat the cost if we miss. Spike has a 3-day walk-away clause — full refund. On Production Sprint the deliverables are written into a one-page scope before money changes hands. If we're not delivering, you stop paying. The structural protection is in the engagement, not in our company size."
"Can you give me a reference?"
"Yes. Doug runs skateOS at 2nd Nature Park; you can walk in, see it running, and ask the front desk. For Branch Manager (tree care), I'll get you in touch with the operator when you're ready. Skip the marketing-deck testimonials — go see the software running in production."
