The real reason people switch

The moment SaaS lock-in actually costs you.

Nobody leaves a known system for marginal improvements. People leave when SaaS becomes a problem — and these are the specific moments they call us. If any of these sound familiar, we should talk.

💸 The price hike

"Mindbody just raised our plan 40%."

Every legacy SaaS does this on a predictable cycle. You have zero leverage because your data is on their server. When you own the software, the only "price hike" is whatever cloud-provider fees naturally do — which is roughly flat or trending down for the underlying infra you'd use.

🏢 The acquisition / pivot

"ServiceTitan got bought / IPO'd. Now what?"

PE firms acquire SaaS companies and squeeze customers. Public companies optimize for quarterly EPS. Either way the product gets worse for SMBs. When you own the software, there's no "now what" — your operation runs identically tomorrow as today, regardless of who's at the top.

🚧 The missing feature

"I've been asking for this for 18 months."

Legacy roadmaps optimize for the median customer. Your specific workflow doesn't make the cut. When you own the software, your missing feature is a 1-week Fix-it Sprint, not a feature-request ticket in a backlog you'll never see again.

🔒 The audit / compliance ask

"Our insurer / partner wants to see where the data lives."

SaaS-vendor data is opaque — you can't show your insurer the schema, your processor's data-handling, where backups live, who has access. When the data lives in YOUR Supabase, the answer is: "on a database we control, with a documented backup policy, and here are the credentials of who can access it."

📈 The growth wall

"We're opening location #2 and the software won't multi-tenant cleanly."

Most legacy SMB SaaS is built single-tenant per account. When you add a location you start juggling two logins, two billing cycles, no unified reporting. Both skateOS and Branch Manager are multi-tenant from the ground up — opening location #2 is a new tenant in the same install, not a new contract.

💰 The annual statement

"Wait. I'm paying how much per year for this?"

Mindbody / Square POS / Jobber bills nickel-and-dime into $5-12k/yr for a mid-size operator. Stack on payment-processing markups and add-on fees. A one-time $15-25k deployment of software you own pays for itself by year 3-4 and is free forever after.

The honest version: if none of these are happening to you yet, stay on what works. We're not interested in churning you off a system that's serving you well. But the day one of these hits — that's the moment we want to be the call you make.

No vendor on earth can hold your data hostage when your data lives on your servers and the source code lives in your git repo.
