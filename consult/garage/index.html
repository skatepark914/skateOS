The SaaS read

The only legally-required thing that changes is sales tax. NY started taxing SaaS as "prewritten software" in 2018, so a NY-based business charging $299/month to a NY customer must register for a Certificate of Authority, collect 8.375% sales tax on top of the fee, and remit it quarterly. Total cost: $0 plus ~5 minutes of paperwork every 90 days.

Where SaaS actually gets risky is liability, not paperwork. If your hosted system goes down and a customer loses revenue, or if you leak customer data, that's when sole-proprietor mode stops being smart. The day you sign your first $299/mo recurring contract is the right day to file an LLC ($200 + ~$200-1500 NY publication) and start looking at E&O + cyber liability insurance ($2-3k/yr combined). Not required by law — but the asymmetric protection is the right price at recurring revenue.

Practical sequence we'd actually follow:

  • 1. First SaaS customer signed → register NY Cert of Authority that week (30 min), start collecting sales tax
  • 2. Same week → write a one-page ToS + Privacy Policy (we already have templates; takes an evening)
  • 3. Second SaaS customer signed → file LLC, get E&O + cyber quote. Don't wait — at 2+ customers the exposure-vs-cost math flips.
  • 4. Any healthcare-adjacent customer → BAA before they get production access.
  • 5. Any EU customer → add a DPA addendum to their ToS.
