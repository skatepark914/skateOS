📐 How the math works (the honest version)

SaaS subscription growth — We project two price hikes of 15% each over 5 years, compounding. This matches the public pricing history of Mindbody, Square POS Plus, ServiceTitan, and Jobber. Light operators may see less; heavy operators routinely see more.

Credit card processing — The "current rate" is your effective rate including all markups your POS vendor adds on top of interchange. Square's published rate is 2.6%+$0.10, but effective rates after add-ons typically land at 2.7-2.9%. The "after switch" rate is what a direct merchant account on interchange-plus pricing typically delivers (Helcim, Payment Cloud, similar). We default to 2.2% which is a conservative middle estimate — real Helcim accounts often run 2.0-2.3% depending on card mix.

Cloud infrastructure — Assumes Supabase Pro ($25/mo), Cloudflare Workers (free tier on low volume, ~$10/mo at scale), Resend ($20/mo), Twilio (~$15/mo for typical SMS volume). Total ~$70/mo paid directly to vendors. Real numbers vary with usage.

Maintenance retainer — Set to $1,500/yr as a middle estimate. Some operators pay $0 (handle updates themselves), some pay $3k/yr (we handle quarterly updates + on-call). Adjust the retainer line in the breakdown to match your expected level.

One-time deploy — Mid-range estimate. skateOS deploy-your-own is $15-25k, Branch Manager is $20-35k. Custom multi-location or industry-specific work runs higher.

What we're NOT modeling — Switching costs (~$2-5k of your time during onboarding), opportunity cost of capital tied up in the deploy fee, value of customizations you can do on owned software that you can't do on SaaS. The first two argue against ownership; the third argues for it. Roughly washes out.

🤔 When does this NOT work?

The math only favors ownership if your current SaaS bill + processing fees are nontrivial. Specifically:

• If you're paying <$100/mo total in software + add-ons AND processing <$250k/yr in card volume, the legacy SaaS bill is too small to justify a $15-25k deploy fee. Stay on what you have.

• If you're a brand-new business and don't yet know what features you need, build on legacy SaaS first. Switch later when the workflow is settled.

• If you have zero technical capability AND don't want to pay a maintenance retainer, the ongoing infrastructure self-management may not work for you. Stay on hosted SaaS (theirs or ours).

• If your industry has hard SOC 2 / HIPAA / PCI requirements that you cannot meet on self-hosted, stay on a vendor that has the attestations.
