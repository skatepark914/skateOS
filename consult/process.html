Hudson Valley AI / Process

How a project works.

Discovery → written scope → kickoff → daily ship cadence → security review → handoff. Concrete sequence, no mystery, accountable at every step.

1

Intro chat

Free · 30 minutes · usually within a day or two

You tell us what you're trying to build or fix. We listen. By the end we either have a clear idea or we tell you we're not the right fit and point you elsewhere.

  • What you're trying to do
  • What you've got so far
  • What your budget & timeline look like
  • Anything sensitive (regulated data, hard deadlines, fragile dependencies)
What you walk away with: A short note from us with what we heard + which engagement model fits + an honest go/no-go.
2

Written plan + simple agreement

A few days after the intro chat

If we're a fit we send you a one-pager: scope, deliverables, milestones, price, timeline. Plus a short engagement letter (not a 20-page MSA). NDA on request, mutual, standard form.

  • One-page scope — exactly what we're going to build, by when, for how much.
  • NDA if you want one — mutual, standard, redlineable.
  • BAA if regulated data is in scope (HIPAA).
What you walk away with: Documents in your inbox you can actually read in 10 minutes.
3

Kickoff

Day 1 of the project · 50% up front

By end of day 1, you have a working URL.

  • Cloud accounts and repos set up in your name (we get scoped access, not ownership).
  • First feature live on a real URL — not "we'll start tomorrow."
  • Whatever chat channel you prefer (Slack, email thread, Signal) for daily updates.
What you walk away with: A real URL, your credentials, our roadmap.
4

Ship daily

Throughout the project

This is the part that's actually different from an agency. We ship features daily. You see progress as it lands, not after a 2-week sprint. If something's off, you tell us in the morning and we fix it that afternoon.

  • Daily updates — short note: what shipped, what's blocked, what's next.
  • Weekly 30-min sync — quick video call to align on priorities for the week.
  • Staging URL with the latest build, reachable any time.
  • Out-of-scope? We write down what changed, what it costs, what it'd push. No surprises.
5

Security look-over

Last week of the project

Jon reviews the build before delivery — the same kind of audit he'd do on any third-party system. Catches what's worth catching before it ships to your users.

  • Code review for the usual suspects (OWASP Top 10, common misconfigurations, hardcoded secrets).
  • External attack-surface check — authentication, IDOR, injection, the obvious stuff.
  • Configuration audit — access rules, secrets, logging, backups.
What you walk away with: A short written report with findings and severity. Critical and high findings fixed before delivery.
6

Handoff

Last week · 50% balance on delivery

You get the keys, not just a URL.

  • Source code — repo transferred to your control, full history.
  • Architecture doc — what's running where, what depends on what.
  • Runbook — how to deploy, how to roll back, what to do if it breaks.
  • Credentials & access — transferred to whoever owns it on your side.
  • Security report from phase 5.
  • 30 days of free post-delivery support — bug fixes, questions, handoff to your team. Included on Production Sprint.
7

Keep us around (optional)

After delivery · month to month

If you want us to keep building or keep an eye on the system after delivery, we can. Month-to-month, cancel any time, no commitment.

  • Fix-it Sprint ($3k) — one focused week to clean something up or extend a feature.
  • Light retainer — ad-hoc help on the system we built, billed by the hour.

For full-time managed-IT or continuous-development partnerships, we'll point you to a managed-services firm — that's a different shape of engagement than what we're set up for.
