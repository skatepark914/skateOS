When you should pick someone else

Honesty matters more than closing every deal. If any of these describe your situation, save us both time and pick the right tool.

🎨

You need a brand-only design firm

If your project is "redesign our logo + brand identity + marketing site" and you don't need code, hire a brand studio. We do design that supports the software we build, but we're not a brand agency.

📱

You need native mobile-first iOS/Android with deep platform features

We build mobile (React Native / Expo) but for highly-platform-specific work — ARKit, deep iOS integrations, App Store optimization at scale — a specialist mobile shop is a better fit.

🧪

You need cutting-edge ML research or training

We integrate AI (Claude, GPT, etc.) into production software very well. We don't train custom models from scratch. For that, find an ML research consultancy.

💰

Your budget is under $3,000

Below that we're not the cheapest path. Try a solo freelancer on Upwork or a no-code platform (Webflow, Bubble, Glide — $0–$300/mo).

🏢

You need a full IT department, 24/7 on-call support, or a formal compliance attestation

Our scope is project-based software + security work. Continuous follow-the-sun managed-services or formal compliance programs are a different shape of engagement. Hire a managed-services firm or a tier-1 consultancy for those.

⏰

You want it built tomorrow with no input from you

Even our Spike package needs you available for ~30 minutes of decisions on day 1. If you can't show up at all, we can't help. Try a fully-managed no-code platform instead.

When you should stay on legacy SaaS

We're not interested in switching customers who are well-served by what they have. Specifically:

📉

You're paying <$100/mo total in software, with low transaction volume

The legacy SaaS bill is too small to justify a $15-25k deploy fee. Stay on what you have. We'll be a fit later when your bill — and your ambition — grows.

🆕

You're a brand-new business still figuring out the workflow

Build on legacy SaaS first. They've solved 80% of the "what does a typical business in this vertical need" question. Once your workflow is settled and you've hit specific limitations, then look at ownership.

🤷

Your current SaaS works fine and isn't actively costing you

If you're not in pain — no price-hike fatigue, no missing-feature frustration, no data-sovereignty concern — there's no urgency to switch. Bookmark us for when the pain shows up.
