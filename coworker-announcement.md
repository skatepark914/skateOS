# Big news — we're building our own system

Subject line options:
- **We're moving off Square — here's what's changing**
- **New park system coming — quick read**
- **Team update: building our own POS (and it's going to be great)**

---

Hey team,

Wanted to loop you in on something I've been working on for the past couple weeks that's going to make all our lives easier at the park.

**Short version**: we're replacing Square with our own system. Same job for you day-to-day, but better tools, way less money going to fees, and features Square literally can't do.

## Why we're making the switch

1. **Square is expensive.** We're paying around **$775/month in card-processing fees** right now, plus software tiers we barely use. At our volume that's real money — roughly **$9,300/year** going to Square.
2. **We don't own our data.** Our customer list, sales history, membership tracking — it all lives inside Square's system. If their prices go up 30% next year (they will), we're stuck.
3. **Square doesn't do what a skate park actually needs.** No real punch cards. No lesson calendar. No birthday-party package bookings. No waiver integration. We've been working around it for years.

## What this means for you

**Mostly nothing is changing about your shift.** You'll still:
- Check people in
- Ring up day passes and retail
- Run the cash drawer
- Process card payments

**What WILL change:**
- **Your own login** (email + password, instead of the shared iPad login) — so we can see who did what, and if someone quits, we deactivate their account, not change the password for everyone
- **Face ID sign-in** on the iPad once we're on the new app — no more typing passwords
- **Faster check-ins** — search a skater by name, tap "Check In," done. Pulls their waiver status from Smartwaiver automatically.
- **Real member QR cards** — kids get a card with a QR code, scan it at the front desk, instant check-in
- **Mobile payments** — Tap to Pay on iPhone means instructors can take payment for a lesson right there at the bowl, no card reader needed

## The money side — monthly ops

Here's what we're currently paying vs. what the new system will cost:

| What we pay for | Square (now) | New system |
|---|---|---|
| Card processing fees | ~$775/mo | ~$525/mo |
| Software / features | $0–165/mo | $25/mo (Supabase) |
| Backups, email, SMS | (bundled) | ~$35/mo |
| Cyber insurance | $0 | ~$50/mo |
| **Total / month** | **~$775–940** | **~$635** |
| **Total / year** | **~$9,300–11,300** | **~$7,600** |
| **Annual savings** | — | **~$1,700–3,700, forever** |

Even better: the savings **grow as we grow**. Every extra $1,000 in revenue through Square costs us another $26 in fees. Through the new system: $8.80.

## Hardware — one-time

Most of what we have carries over. We're only replacing what's locked to Square.

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Helcim Smart Terminal | $329 | Replaces Square Reader / Stand |
| Initial member QR cards (500) | $60 | Printed + laminated |
| iPad stand (generic) | $40 | If we keep our iPad off the Square Stand |
| **Total hardware** | **~$430** | |

**What we keep**: the iPad, the receipt printer (if it's Epson or Star — generic, works with anything), the cash drawer (it's a dumb metal box that kicks when the printer tells it to), any barcode scanner.

**What we sell on eBay after the switch**: Square Stand (~$80), Square Reader (~$25 each), Square Terminal if we have one (~$150).

**Net hardware cost after reselling**: ~$200–300 all-in. That's one week of processing-fee savings.

## How it's going to roll out

No "flip the switch and hope" — we're doing this carefully.

- **Now → early June**: I'm building the system. You'll see some changes happen quietly in the background.
- **Early-to-mid June**: **Soft launch** — we run the new system alongside Square for a couple weeks. Reconcile the totals at the end of each day. Fix anything weird.
- **Late June / early July**: Once we're confident, we unplug Square.
- **Training**: ~15 minutes per person. It genuinely looks and feels simpler than Square.
