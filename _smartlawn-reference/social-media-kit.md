### Pre-Seeded Q&A (10 questions)

Seed these questions and answers on your Google Business listing:

**Q: Do robotic mowers work on hills?**
A: Yes. The Navimow models handle slopes up to 45% (about 24 degrees). The i2 AWD and X4 series have all-wheel drive specifically designed for hilly terrain, which is common in Westchester and Putnam County. We assess your property's terrain during our free consultation.

**Q: Do I need to install a boundary wire?**
A: No. All Navimow models use RTK satellite navigation or LiDAR -- no buried wires needed. You set boundaries through the smartphone app using a virtual map. Installation is faster and there's nothing to dig up.

**Q: How much does installation cost?**
A: Our Basic installation starts at $299 for properties up to 1/4 acre, Standard installation is $599 for up to 1/2 acre, and Premium is $999 for up to 1 acre. We handle everything: charging station, app configuration, boundary setup, zone programming, and owner training.

**Q: What happens in winter?**
A: We offer a Winterization service ($149) that includes full cleaning, battery conditioning, charging station prep, and storage guidance. If you have an annual service plan, winterization is included. The Yarbo S1 can also handle snow removal with its modular snow blower attachment.

**Q: Is Mowing as a Service available in my area?**
A: MaaS is available throughout Westchester and Putnam County. We serve Peekskill, Cortlandt, Yorktown Heights, Croton-on-Hudson, Buchanan, Mohegan Lake, Mahopac, Carmel, and surrounding areas. Call (914) 391-5233 to check availability at your address.

**Q: Can I see a demo before buying?**
A: Absolutely. We offer free on-site demos where we bring a mower to your property and let you see it in action on your own lawn. Call (914) 391-5233 or fill out the form at smartlawnny.com to schedule.

**Q: Do you service mowers you didn't sell?**
A: We primarily service Navimow and Yarbo mowers. If you purchased one elsewhere, we can still provide maintenance, blade changes, and seasonal care. Contact us to discuss your specific model.

**Q: How loud are robotic mowers?**
A: Robotic mowers operate at about 55-60 decibels -- quieter than a normal conversation. You can run them at any hour, including early morning or late evening, without disturbing neighbors. Most people can't hear them from inside their house.

**Q: How long do robotic mowers last?**
A: With proper maintenance, a Navimow or Yarbo mower will last 8-10+ years. The battery typically lasts 5-7 years before needing replacement. Our annual service plans keep your mower running at peak performance throughout its life.

**Q: What size lawn can a robotic mower handle?**
A: Our lineup covers lawns from 0.15 acres up to 2.25 acres with a single mower. The Navimow i2 AWD handles up to 1/4 acre, the H2 up to 1/2 acre, the X4 up to 1.25 acres, and the X390 up to 2.25 acres. For larger properties, we offer multi-mower setups.

## 8. OG META TAGS FOR WEBSITE

Add these tags to the `<head>` section of both `index.html` and `services.html` for proper link previews when your site is shared on social media:

```html
<!-- Open Graph / Facebook -->
<meta property="og:type" content="website">
<meta property="og:url" content="https://smartlawnny.com/">
<meta property="og:title" content="Smart Lawn NY | Robotic Mower Dealership & Service Center">
<meta property="og:description" content="The Hudson Valley's first dedicated robotic mower dealership. Authorized Navimow & Yarbo dealer. Sales, installation & service in Peekskill, NY.">
<meta property="og:image" content="https://smartlawnny.com/og-share.png">

<!-- Twitter -->
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">
<meta name="twitter:title" content="Smart Lawn NY | Robotic Mower Dealership">
<meta name="twitter:description" content="Robotic mower sales, installation & service. Authorized Navimow & Yarbo dealer. Peekskill, NY.">
<meta name="twitter:image" content="https://smartlawnny.com/og-share.png">
```

Note: After downloading the OG share image from social-graphics.html, rename it to `og-share.png` and place it in your site folder. Then redeploy to Netlify.
